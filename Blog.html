Books I've Read

12 Rules for Life:

an antidote for chaos 10/10

This book is written by Jordan Peterson. He is a Canadian psycologist who has his own practice, has taught at University of Toronto in Canada, and Harvard University in the United States. The main point of the book is that you are the reason you have all your problems but at the same time you are able to fix it by taking responsibility. Some of the chapters include: "Treat Yourself Like Someone You Are Responsible For Helping", "Compare Yourself to Who You Were Yesterday, Not To Who Someone Else Is Today", "Set Your House In Order Before You Criticize The World" and "Assume That The Person You Are Listening to Might Know Something You Don't". He also has a lot of his speaking engagements on YouTube as well.

Measure What Matters:

How Google, Bono, and the Gates Foundation Rock the World with OKRs9/10

This book is written by John Doerr. He is a venture capitalist at Kleiner Perkins in Menlo Park, CA. They specialize in helping early stage tech companies get started including Amazon, Google, Sun Microsystems, along with many other high profile names. In this book he talks about how to be more productive and making sure you aren't mistaking progress for opportunity. They talk about how major compaines used OKRs, which stands for Objectives and Key Results, to be more productive and be able to scale quickly. How this helps is having an objective or big goal, and deciding on a very specific short term goals as that will lead you to complete the large goal. This way people who use this system have self feedback quickly so that they can see what they need to adjust to make sure the larger goal is finished as close to on time as possible.

The Rise of Superman:

Decoding the Science of Ultimate Human Performance 9/10

This book is written by Steven Kotler. He is the founder of the Flow Research Collective and is also an award-winning journalist. In the book they talk about how athletes, especially extreme athletes use the state of "Flow" to increase their performance and do near impossible feats. He then relates it back normal people and how they can use the same state of flow to increase their performance.
